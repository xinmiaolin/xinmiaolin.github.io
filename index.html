About Me

I am a Ph.D. student (2019-) at B. Thomas Golisano College of Computing and Information Sciences (GCCIS) of Rochester Institute of Technology (R.I.T), working with Prof. Yu Kong and Prof. Qi Yu. Prior to RIT, I received my Master's degree (2016-2019) and Bachelor's degree (2012-2016) at the School of Remote Sensing and Information Engineering, Wuhan University (WHU). In WHU, my research was advised by Prof. Daiqin Yang and Prof. Zhenzhong Chen at the Lab. of Intelligent Information Processing (IIP).

My research goal is to achieve visual intelligence for open-world applications. I am interested in solving large-scale visual recognition and prediction problems in an open world by novel machine learning and deep learning approaches. My research experience includes but not limited to traffic accident anticipation, action recognition, 2D/3D object detection, human visual attention, and remote sensing. Currently, I am interested in developing trustworthy, interpretable, and robust AI models to address the video-based decision-making problems.
